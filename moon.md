# The Moon
The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth-largest moon in the Solar System and is commonly thought to have formed 4.5 billion years ago, after a Mars-sized object collided with Earth.

## Basic Facts
- **Distance from Earth**: Approximately 384,400 km
- **Diameter**: About 3,474 km
- **Composition**: Primarily rock and metal, with a crust, mantle, and core.

## Phases of the Moon
The Moon goes through a cycle of phases due to its position relative to the Earth and Sun. The main phases include:
- New Moon
- First Quarter
- Full Moon
- Last Quarter

## Influence on Earth
- **Tides**: The Moon's gravitational pull causes ocean tides.
- **Stabilizing Earth's Axial Tilt**: The Moon helps stabilize Earth's axial tilt, contributing to a stable climate.

## Interesting Facts
- The same side of the Moon always faces Earth due to tidal locking.
- The far side of the Moon was first seen by humans during the Apollo missions.
- The Moon has no atmosphere, leading to extreme temperature variations.
